Basis forms a network of people actively engaging in a post-capitalist economy with one another.

The network acts as a foundation for members to provide goods and services to each other without the profit motive.
Read more »
Property in the network is shared by all members. Housing, factories, office space, and machinery are managed by the people using them.
Read more »
The needs of the members are met by a Basic Income that grows into a living wage as the network becomes more self-sufficient.

{% include leader.html text="How Basis works: Part 2 - Production without profit" image="/assets/images/port.jpg" id="production-without-profit" large=true %}

Basis creates an economy where people are paid for producing things rather than selling things. This shift organizes the productive system around meeting needs rather than generating profit.

Companies in Basis don't use money, but manage costs directly. Costs are tracked as distinct sets of labor and resources.
A company assigns its costs to its products. When another company orders one of those products, it takes on the associated cost.
When a product is finally sold to a consumer, the difference between the price and the cost is absorbed by the network.

{% include leader.html text="How Basis works: Part 4 - Meeting basic needs" image="/assets/images/market.jpg" id="meeting-basic-needs" large=true %}

A living wage basic income is provided to everyone in the network to meet the needs of all members.

Members get basic income whether they are working or not, freeing them from the need to sell themselves for economic survival.
Having basic needs covered outside of production means all products become cheaper and the economy can be adaptable and fluid.
Because credits in Basis do not circulate after they are spent, basic income can be provided to everyone without taxation.
