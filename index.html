What is Basis?

Basis is a vision and implementation for what might come after capitalism. It strives to achieve the free association of producers and uses in-kind cost tracking as a tool for ecologically-focused production. The project incorporates a set of open-source tools for creating and managing a bottom-up productive system where profit is no longer used in production, property is shared, and the basic needs of all participants are met.

What are the project's goals?

  1. Transparent ecological production. Capitalism has failed us and our planet. While it provides the things we need, it demands that we work harder and harder to produce things we absolutely don't need, and in the process ravages the planet and its resources. Basis envisions a productive system where need, not profit, is the primary objective of any economic activity. Beyond that, Basis seeks to make the relationship between our productive system and our environment open information for anyone to see, down to the individual product.
  2. Shared property. The concept of absentee ownership is rampant in our culture. Houses sit empty while families sleep in the streets. Workers provide the value while shareholders make all the descisions. Basis seeks a more intentful approach to the concept of property, such that determining use of housing, factories, and other property is removed from market forces, but more importantly that the people most affected by a resource are the ones who manage it. We envision a world without landlords or private owners.
  3. Free association. Our economy often forces us into places we don't want to be. How many of us have had to work jobs where at best we see no purpose in what we were doing, and at worst we are subject to abuse? Why do we spend so much of our time doing things that have no meaning to us or to others? Basis seeks to meet the basic needs of all participants, freeing them from having to constantly sell themselves, and allowing society to organize around meaning and purpose rather than making money.

Why build this?

As capitalism has given to us, it has also taken away. It gives us a wage but takes our sense of ownership and purpose. It gives us houses but forces us into debt to live in them. It gives us hospitals but bankrupts us when we use them. It hands our political self-determination to banks and corporations and placates us with an endless stream of consumer trinkets, all while telling us we have "choice."

The goal of Basis is to enable a system where we keep our sense of ownership and purpose, we keep the houses and hospitals we have built, we keep our self-determination, but shed the perversion of capital and the alienation it brings with it.

Imagine a world where instead of asking "Will this make money?" people ask "Will this be useful to others?" It's a small shift in thinking, but would profoundly change our relationships to production, the environment, and each other. What would the world look like if we felt a connection to not just the things we made, but with the meaning behind our work? Basis was built with the goal of enabling an economy where profit is removed from production, replacing the random coldness of markets with a sense of connected purpose.
